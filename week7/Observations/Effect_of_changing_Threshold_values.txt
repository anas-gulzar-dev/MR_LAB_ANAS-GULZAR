1. Front Distance Setting (When to Dodge)
This setting controls how close the robot can get to a wall in front of it before it stops and turns.

Testing at 0.5 meters (Further away)

The Good: It reacted early, providing a large safety margin.

The Bad: It was too cautious and would often "dodge" invisible obstacles in wide-open hallways.

Testing at 0.2 meters (Very close)

The Good: It stopped dodging nothing in open spaces and only reacted to real, immediate threats.

The Bad: It waited too long to react. It was so close to the wall that sometimes it would crash before the turn actually started.

Recommendation: A setting of 0.35 meters is the sweet spot—safe enough to avoid crashes, but close enough to avoid unnecessary twitching in open spaces.

2. Side Distance Setting (When to Follow the Wall)
This setting controls when the robot decides it is close enough to a wall on its side to start driving parallel to it.

Testing at 0.5 meters (Further away)

What happened: The robot started following walls easily and stayed nicely centered in the hallways.

Testing at 0.2 meters (Very close)

What happened: The robot would only start following the wall if it was practically touching it. Because it rarely got that close, it mostly just drove straight and ignored the walls.

The result: This setting basically broke the wall-following feature.
