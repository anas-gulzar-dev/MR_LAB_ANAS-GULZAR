1. Sensor Test (Standing Still)
When the robot was just scanning (not moving), its sensors correctly measured how far away the walls were:

Front: About 0.9 meters away

Left: About 1.2 meters away

Right: About 0.6 meters away

2. Driving Test (Dodging Obstacles)
When we let the robot drive itself, it successfully spotted obstacles. When it got too close to a wall in front of it, it would stop and turn toward whichever side had more open space.

We tested two different distance settings to tell the robot when it should start turning away from a wall:

Setting 1: Turning at 0.2 meters (Very close)

What happened: The robot drove right up to the walls before turning.

The result: It could easily navigate tight spaces, but it was risky because it got dangerously close to crashing before it reacted.

Setting 2: Turning at 0.5 meters (Further away)

What happened: The robot started turning much earlier.

The result: It was much safer and had plenty of room to turn. However, it was a bit overly cautious and would start turning even when there was plenty of open space ahead, which slowed down its progress.
